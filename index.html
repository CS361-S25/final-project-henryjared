About Daisyworld

Daisyworld is a hypothetical model introduced by James Lovelock (1983) to illustrate how life can influence a planet's environment to maintain habitable conditions. In this simulator, black and white daisies with different albedos grow and die based on local temperatures, which they also affect by altering the planet's reflectivity. The feedback between daisy populations and climate demonstrates a simple yet powerful example of biological regulation of planetary temperature-highlighting how life and environment can co-evolve in balance.

Our version extends the original Daisyworld by introducing latitude-based temperature variation and a new gray daisy type with intermediate reflectivity. Explore how changes in daisy types, sunlight, and planetary conditions influence habitability. We encourage you to experiment with the settings; see how life adapts, survives, or fails under different scenarios!
